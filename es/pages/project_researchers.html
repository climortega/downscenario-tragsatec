Project Researchers

IRENE RODRÍGUEZ MUÑOZ

Bachelor’s Degree in Physics by the Complutense University of Madrid, with a specialization in Atmospheric Physics.

This researcher has extensive experience in meteorological observation and data analysis, instrumentation handling, and development of applications and algorithms based on satellite data.

Her work on this project has focused on relative humidity evaluation and downscaling, implementation and verification of downscaling for seasonal forecasting, visualization efforts, and result application to the AquaCrop model.

JESÚS GUTIÉRREZ FERNÁNDEZ

PhD in Agricultural and Environmental Sciences by the University of Castilla-La Mancha, with a dissertation focused on Mediterranean cyclones. Bachelor’s Degree in Environmental Sciences by the University of Castilla-La Mancha, with a Master’s Degree in Meteorology and Geophysics by the Complutense University of Madrid.

This researcher has extensive experience in the study of cyclones and medicanes, as well as extreme events such as heat waves and heavy rainfall.

His work on this project has focused on temperature and precipitation evaluation and downscaling, study of extreme events for precipitation patterns, and result application to water reservoirs.

MARÍA ORTEGA CAMACHO

PhD in Agricultural and Environmental Sciences by the University of Castilla-La Mancha, with a dissertation focused on small-scale winds. Bachelor’s Degree in Chemistry by the University of Murcia, with a Master’s Degree in Environmental Sustainability by the University of Castilla-La Mancha.

This researcher has extensive experience in the study of regional winds, in the use of downscaling techniques and high-resolution products, and in research for wind energy development.

Their work on this project has focused on wind speed and short-wave radiation evaluation and downscaling, implementation and verification of downscaling for seasonal forecasting, and web development.
