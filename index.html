ABOUT

The main objective of this project is to provide high-resolution climate information for the Iberian Peninsula by applying statistical downscaling to key climate variables under available climate change scenarios.

Statistical downscaling links large-scale climate model outputs with local observations, making it possible to obtain more detailed and locally relevant climate projections.

To achieve this, five essential variables were selected: temperature, precipitation, wind speed, relative humidity, and short-wave surface radiation. Since local observations are limited for some of them, several high-resolution reanalysis datasets were evaluated to identify the one that best matches AEMET observations.

Finally, statistical downscaling was applied to these five variables using an extended ensemble of eleven global climate models and providing robust and comprehensive future climate projections for the Iberian Peninsula.

PROJECT TEAM & FUNDING

This project was funded through a service contract in which Tragsatec (Tragsa Group) acted as a subcontractor for the Climate Evaluation and Modeling Area at the Spanish State Meteorological Agency (AEMET).
