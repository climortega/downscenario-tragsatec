METHODS

The Iberian Peninsula is one of the Mediterranean regions most prone to prolonged droughts. These periods of low rainfall necessitate large water reserves for storage. In recent years, several climate change studies have identified the Iberian Peninsula as one of the regions on the planet experiencing the greatest decline in rainfall. Therefore, the results of this project have been applied, using previously obtained downscaled precipitation data (see Downscaling section), to explore changes in future rainfall patterns for the 1985-2020 period (November to March).

To this end, correlation between precipitation in river basins (see Data section) and CERRA-Land was calculated. Then, the PLS (partial least squares regression) method was applied. PLS is a multiple linear regression method consisting of two parts:

  1. Selection of precipitation predictors. A correlation study was performed between precipitation predicted by the model and actual reservoir inflow data, and reservoirs with average correlations greater than 0.7 were selected (127 reservoirs).

  2. Training. It was performed using CERRA-Land. A linear regression model was created from these 127 data points and applied to the previously regionalized data.

The results of this model were subsequently studied.
