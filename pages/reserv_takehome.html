  • CERRA-Land reasonably reproduces inflow reservoir data observed during evaluation period with a correlation greater than 0.9. Therefore, it has been chosen as the basis for calibrating the inflow model with PLS (100 predictors), which provides a practical and reproducible method capable of extrapolating to downscaled climate change scenarios in order to obtain detailed information.

  • Downscaled CMIP6 models show similar means to CERRA-Land and consistency in mean values when transferring the function calibrated with reanalysis.

  • A uniform downward trend in inflow data does not appear in the future climate.

  • There are some notable spatial patterns, such as a decrease in contributions in the southwest of the Iberian Peninsula and a tendency to increase in the northwest (areas near Galicia).

  • Overall, no significant changes in percentiles (extremes) are observed. There is a slight decrease in the 95th percentile towards the end of the 21st century, which may indicate a possible reduction in high-inflow events.

María Ortega, Irene Rodríguez-Muñoz, Jesús Gutiérrez-Fernández (Tragsatec) ● Esteban Rodríguez-Guisado (AEMET)
