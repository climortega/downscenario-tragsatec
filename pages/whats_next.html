What's Next?

DEEPER STUDY OF DOWNSCALING METHODOLOGIES

Downscaling methodologies used in this project warrant further investigation, as some offered by pyClim-SDM software, such as analogue methods, have not been employed, while others have yielded inconclusive results. DeepESD, in particular, is a source of great interest to researchers. Given the good results obtained for temperature and precipitation with their respective models (DeepESDtas and DeepESDpr), it is possible that developing models for wind, humidity, and radiation (variables to which the temperature model has been applied due to time constraints) could also return better conclusions. However, internal evaluations have concluded that adding other predictors (such as the predicting variable itself) does not significantly improve the flawed calculations, so perhaps changing the model would not help either. In any case, further research into downscaling methods, their application to other emission scenarios, and the generation of results for dissemination to the general public are necessary and could not be carried out in this project due to lack of time, so they would be of great interest for future work.

FURTHER INVESTIGATION OF EXTREME PRECIPITATION EVENTS

Extreme precipitation events are of great importance to the general population of the Iberian Peninsula due to several phenomena that have affected people in recent years and their increased intensity as a consequence of climate change. Furthermore, the rise in droughts could also impact the population in other ways, such as water scarcity. Therefore, further study of these phenomena is needed, and downscaled datasets allow for a more detailed investigation than previously possible.

COMPLEMENTARY WIND STUDIES

Due to time constraints, this study did not include a more in-depth analysis of the wind variable. However, wind is of particular interest in terms of downscaling, as airflows depend heavily on the orographic and detailed characteristics of the terrain, leading to the generation of characteristic regional winds, many of which are very intense. Future work aims to develop a detailed downscaling of wind patterns and a thorough study of its extremes (both intense phenomena and low-wind droughts), and its application to fields of interest such as renewable energy generation.

IMPLEMENTATION OF AQUACROP WITH CURRENT DOWNSCALED CLIMATE SCENARIOS

AquaCrop crop model was intended to be run using downscaled results obtained in this project. However, due to time constraints, it has been programmed to run with downscaled atmospheric data from AdapteCCa (CMIP5 project, and therefore slightly older than the data currently being generated). In the future, work will continue with AquaCrop to obtain up-to-date, high-quality, and very high-resolution crop data, which is of great interest to the general public, through deep learning methods. The workflow will also be generalized to provide this data for a wide range of crops and different emission scenarios.
