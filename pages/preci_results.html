PRECIPITATION EXTREMES - RESULTS

Studying the distribution of hourly precipitation curves reveals that all reanalysis overestimates values, particularly ERA5 and ERA5-Land. COSMO-REA6 fits the distribution curve better. Although some discrepancies exist between reanalysis and observations, these decrease with increasing accumulation, especially for COSMO-REA6 and CERRA.

Regarding the 95th percentile (extreme precipitation values), COSMO-REA6 is the reanalysis that least underestimates hourly precipitation. ERA5 and ERA5-Land underestimate extreme values more (around 38%). CERRA also underestimates values across all accumulations, but not as much as ERA5 and ERA5-Land.

In the Mediterranean region, the highest values are found in Catalonia, Valencia, and the Andalusian Mediterranean coast. Among the reanalyses, we also find significant differences in the most extreme precipitation values. For example, ERA5 and ERA5-Land did not observe high 95th percentile values in areas of Valencia, while COSMO-REA6 showed values closest to the observational data. However, all agreed on high 95th percentile values in areas of Catalonia and the Andalusian Mediterranean coast.

To illustrate these results, two episodes of extreme rainfall that occurred on the Mediterranean coast of the Iberian Peninsula have been selected.

Between October 20 and 25, 2000, an episode of extreme rainfall affected large areas of the Spanish Mediterranean coast, particularly the Valencian Community. Reanalysis shows significant differences in the intensity and extent of rainfall during this period, with CERRA showing the highest intensity. It is followed by COSMO-REA6, where the accumulated rainfall is greater in Andalusia. ERA5 and ERA5-Land underestimate rainfall but show maximum amounts in areas of the Valencian Community.

On the other hand, between October 28 and November 2, 2024 (especially October 29), a historic episode of torrential rainfall occurred, affecting the entire Spanish Mediterranean region. Comparing daily observational data from October 29, 2024 from AEMET stations to reanalysis product values reveals a significant underestimation of the reanalysis data, particularly those from ERA5 and ERA5-Land. CERRA displays the results closest to the observations.

Heaviest rainfall was recorded in areas near Valencia (700 mm in Turís). All reanalysis models underestimate precipitation. CERRA presents the spatial distribution of rainfall closest to observational data, while ERA5 and ERA5-Land do not accurately reproduce the precipitation recorded in the central Iberian Peninsula.

María Ortega, Irene Rodríguez-Muñoz, Jesús Gutiérrez-Fernández (Tragsatec) ● Esteban Rodríguez-Guisado (AEMET)
