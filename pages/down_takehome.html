  • Of all downscaling methods, QDM and DeepESD yield the best results for most variables; in some cases, XGB also performs well. DeepESD is particularly interesting due to its deep learning methodology, which is rarely applied to variables such as wind or humidity.

  • Model performance is highly dependent on each variable. Therefore, using a large ensemble of 11 members is advisable to obtain the most comprehensive information possible.

  • DeepESD provides good downscaling results for temperature, precipitation, and mean ranges.

  • DeepESD also presents some problems for certain variables, mainly in radiation trends and some long-term wind and relative humidity indices.

  • Its main limitation is the use of a temperature model (DeepESDtas) for humidity, radiation, and wind. Developing a specific model for each variable could improve these results.

  • While long-term trends present challenges, short-term trends appear to perform much better. Therefore, this methodology could be applied to other fields, such as seasonal forecasting downscaling.
